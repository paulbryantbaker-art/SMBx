Four ways to make the journey pages pull visitors into the chat.

The current /sell, /buy, /raise, /integrate pages read as marketing scrolls with occasional chat moments. These four reframings each commit harder to the "chat‑first, the page is the product" thesis — same Glass Grok vocabulary, different posture. Using /sell as the working example; structure transfers to the other three. Scroll inside each artboard.

01 Live Deal Room Split screen

Sticky chat rail on the left with a live scripted Yulia thread. The right side is a scrollable workbench — add-back schedule, CIM preview, IOI matrix — that reacts to where you are in the chat. The marketing copy is the UI. Feels like you're already using the product.

02 Conversation Canvas Scroll = dialogue

The whole page is one long chat thread — Yulia pitching the product to you, answering the objections you haven't asked yet. Scroll-spy marker on the right tells you where you are. You can jump in at any point by tapping a message. No brochure sections, no zigzag heroes — just a conversation that sells.

03 Deal Ticker Proof-in-motion

A live, anonymized feed of deals that just closed, CIMs that just shipped, rundowns that just ran. Visitor can tap any row to "replay" what Yulia did. The ticker becomes the product demo. Sticky chat pill at the bottom. Strong social proof, high motion, close to Bloomberg‑terminal in spirit.

04 Filmic Anchors Editorial

Maximum restraint. A handful of oversized anchors — "$1.1M.", "Three weeks ago you wouldn't have known." — each with a single chat prompt attached. Between anchors, full-bleed monochrome mockups. The scroll rhythm is cinematic. For visitors who need to feel the stakes before they ask anything.
