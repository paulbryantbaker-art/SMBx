## Design Context

### Users
Business owners, brokers, sponsors, advisors, and investors navigating M&A transactions from $300K to mega-cap. They arrive with high stakes — selling a business they built, buying one they'll bet their capital on, raising funds that determine their trajectory, or running PMI after close. Context shifts: mobile-first when checking deals between meetings (the broker's pocket), desktop for deep financial analysis (canvas + chat rail). The job: get from "I'm thinking about a deal" to "we closed" with AI-guided intelligence at every gate.

### Product Architecture (so design decisions live in the right place)
- **Front (marketing / journey routes)** — sales copy + interaction. User is anonymous. Every CTA routes to chat — no contact forms, no dead-end pages. This is where editorial reading happens; the rest of the product is the work.
- **Chat (Yulia)** — the gateway. Anonymous users can talk freely; logged-in users continue the same conversation inside the app shell.
- **Internal app (post-login)** — `AppShellInner → TopBar + TabContent (Deal / Docs / Pipeline / Search) + YuliaAgent + TabBar`. Two-pane: **canvas** (work surface, tabbed) + **chat rail** (Yulia, persistent). This is the app.
- **Mobile is a separate front-end** — never share a div with conditional styles. Desktop = Grok + Canva. Mobile = Grok + Apple Liquid Glass (translucent floating chrome, bottom pill, collapsing headers).

### Brand Personality
**Considered, warm, credible.** Yulia is part brilliant deal advisor, part editor-in-chief of a financial publication — she knows the structure cold AND can write a tight memo about it. The platform feels like a boutique M&A firm that happens to be software, with the editorial polish of *The New Yorker* — not a SaaS dashboard with finance features bolted on.

### Emotional Goals
- **Trust** — financial data is sacred, never invented, always sourced
- **Clarity** — complex M&A concepts made accessible without dumbing down
- **Momentum** — every interaction moves the deal forward, never feels like busywork
- **Craft** — every page reads like a printed publication, not a generated artifact. Restraint is the differentiator

### Voice & Tone
- **Startup energy is welcome** — punchy, confident, fast. We're not a stuffy white-shoe firm; we're the platform replacing them.
- **Never sound AI.** No "As an AI…" No "seamless." No "leverage," "unlock," "empower," "delve," "robust." No bullet-list answers when prose would do. No exclamation points unless someone is genuinely excited.
- **Yulia speaks like someone who has read the deal and can write a tight memo about it.** Specific over generic. Numbers over adjectives. One sentence over three.
- **Adapts persona by league tier** — Main Street informal, Lower-Middle Market crisp, Mid-Market polished, Mega-Cap institutional.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Cowork DL — warm editorial.** Color palette and typographic feel sourced from `claude.com/product/cowork` and Anthropic's brand CSS. Cream canvas (`#faf9f5`) with a single bold Clay accent (`#d97757`). Editorial serif (Instrument Serif) for display, humanist sans (Figtree) for body, JetBrains Mono for data. Restrained: most of the interface lives in warm neutrals; Clay appears sparingly (primary CTAs, focus rings, active tab, Yulia "live" dot, send button).

**Full-bleed editorial — *The New Yorker* as the reading reference.** Typographic hierarchy carries the design. Section backgrounds bleed edge-to-edge where intentional (cover plate, dateline strips, pricing). Generous margins, drop caps on the lede, italic serif foil mixed into sans headlines, fleurons as section dividers, mono datelines.

**Known craft challenge — full-bleed spacing drift.** On viewports wider than ~1440px, full-bleed sections sprawl: hero text on the far left, cover plate on the far right, 2000px+ of canvas. Solution lives in `client/src/index.css` — cap section content at ~1200px while letting the background bleed edge-to-edge: `padding-inline: max(56px, calc(50% - 600px))`. Apply this pattern to any new full-bleed section. Don't let editorial composition collapse on big monitors.

**Theme.** Both light and dark, with full Safari toolbar tinting support. Dark mode is warm charcoal `#1a1918`, never blue-black, never pure `#000`. First-class citizen, not an afterthought. Tested at every breakpoint.

### Anti-References

**Visual:**
- Generic SaaS dashboards with too many colored cards
- Robinhood-style gamification
- Anything that looks like a template
- Glassmorphism on content cards (allowed only on floating mobile chrome)
- Purple-blue gradients (the AI-slop tell)
- Pure black `#000` or pure white `#fff` for primary surfaces
- Pink / rose-gold `#D44A78` (dead — superseded by Clay)
- Italic sans (italic only on serif)
- Monospace as decorative "technical" shorthand (reserve for tabular data + timestamps + datelines)
- Rainbow badges (stay in the earth-tone palette)
- Sora, Inter, Roboto, Poppins, Montserrat, Caveat fonts — all dead
- Gradient X logo — dead. Logo is plain text `smbx.ai` in Figtree 700.

**Tonal / copy:**
- AI-flavored phrasing ("seamless," "leverage," "unlock," "empower," "delve," "robust," "as an AI…")
- Bullet-list answers when prose would do
- Exclamation points used as decoration
- Consultant jargon without substance
- Stock-photo handshake energy

### Typography
- **Display**: `Instrument Serif 400`, letter-spacing `-0.01em`, line-height 1.15, `text-wrap: balance`
- **Body**: `Figtree 400`, line-height 1.5, `text-wrap: pretty`, max ~65ch line length
- **UI labels**: `Figtree 500/600`, letter-spacing `-0.005em`
- **Data / numbers**: `Figtree tabular-nums` for inline; `JetBrains Mono` for dense tables, timestamps, datelines, eyebrows (10–11px tracking 0.16em uppercase)
- **Italic**: only on serif (Instrument Serif italic 400 is the editorial foil mixed into sans hero/H2 lines). Never italic sans.
- **Hero scale**: 124px on cover hero h1 / Closer h2 (line 0.86, tracking -0.044em). Section H2: 52–68px. Cap title: 28–34px. Lede: 18px. Body: 15–17px. Eyebrow: 10–11px.

- **Cards**: `#ffffff` / `#1f1e1d`
- **Subtle**: `#f5f4ed` / `#262624` (chip fill, panel bg)
- **Muted / hover**: `#f0eee6`
- **Borders**: `#e8e6dc` (default) / `#dedcd1` (strong)
- **Text**: `#1a1918` ink / `#3d3d3a` body / `#87867f` muted
- **Accent — Clay**: `#d97757` (primary), `#c96442` (hover / pressed). `clay-soft = rgba(217,119,87,0.12)`, `clay-glow = rgba(217,119,87,0.22)`. Dark mode bumps soft → 0.16, glow → 0.28.
- **Terra accent — exhaustive list of where Clay/Terra appears (do not add more):**
  1. Period after "faster" in cover h1
  2. Period after each Spine pull-quote line
  3. Persona quick-pick `↗` glyphs in chat rail
  4. Send button when composer has draft
  5. Cover hero CTA fill
  6. Yulia status pulse-dot
  7. "● drafting" / "● financeable" status dots in CIM/SOP artifacts
  8. "↙" arrow in Closer CTA
  9. 2px top rule on Pro pricing tier

- **Earth-tone palette (content only — badges, tags, charts, score bands, NEVER chrome):** cactus `#bcd1ca`, mineral `#629987`, olive `#788c5d`, oat `#e3dacc`, peach `#ebc9b7`, coral `#ebcece`, fig `#c46686`, plum `#827dbd`, heather `#cbcadb`, sky `#6a9bcc`
- **Never** pure `#000000` or `#ffffff` for primary surfaces. **Never** decorative gradients. Clay-only gradient (`--clay → --clay-interactive`) if a gradient is needed.

### Motion
- Animate only `transform` and `opacity` (GPU-composited)
- Never `transition: all` — list specific properties
- Honor `prefers-reduced-motion` always; provide static beautiful alternatives
- **No bounce, no elastic** — this is finance + editorial, not a toy
- Custom easing curves over CSS defaults: ease-out `cubic-bezier(0.23, 1, 0.32, 1)`; ease-in-out `cubic-bezier(0.77, 0, 0.175, 1)`; iOS drawer `cubic-bezier(0.32, 0.72, 0, 1)`
- **Never `ease-in` on UI** — feels sluggish at the moment users are watching most closely
- Micro-interactions: `active:scale-[0.97]` on buttons (not 0.95 — too much), 100–160ms button feedback, 200ms tool popups, 300ms theme transitions
- UI animation duration cap: **300ms**. Exit animations ~70% of enter duration.
- Popovers: `transform-origin` from trigger (use Radix `--radix-popover-content-transform-origin`). Modals exempt — keep centered.
- Never animate from `scale(0)` — start from `scale(0.95) opacity:0`. Nothing in real life appears from nothing.

### Accessibility
- WCAG 2.1 AA across the board
- All interactive elements keyboard-accessible with visible Clay focus rings
- Contrast: 4.5:1 body text, 3:1 large text + UI components
- Touch targets: minimum 44px on mobile, 8px+ spacing between
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`, `prefers-color-scheme`, system text scaling
- No hover-only affordances on touch surfaces
- **No long-press gestures anywhere** — visible ⋯ menus, swipe actions, or dedicated buttons. (See memory: `feedback_no_long_press.md`.)
- Color is never the only indicator (icons + text accompany state colors)

### Design Principles

1. **Substance over decoration.** Every pixel earns its place. If it doesn't inform, orient, or enable action — remove it. Clay is for primary CTAs and active states, never atmosphere.

2. **Financial precision is non-negotiable.** Tabular nums on every number column. Right-align currency. Never round to clean numbers (47.2%, not 50%). Money stored in cents (integers), displayed with proper formatting. **Zero hallucination on financial data** — extract exactly from documents, never invent.

3. **Mobile is the real product.** Design for the phone in a broker's pocket first. Desktop is the expanded view, not the other way around. Mobile and desktop are **separate front-ends** — never share a div with conditional styles. Touch targets 44px minimum. Portal mobile fixed chrome to `document.body` — ancestor `transform`/`filter`/`backdrop-filter` creates a containing block and breaks `position:fixed`.

4. **Documents are the deliverable.** PDFs, valuations, CIMs, LOIs, SOPs — these are what users pay for. They must look like they came from a boutique advisory firm, not an AI tool. **"Would Goldman Sachs send this?"** is the test. Always in-browser review/edit/approve — never require download to interact.

5. **Yulia is the interface.** Chat is the front door. Every CTA routes to conversation, never to forms or dashboards. The AI doesn't assist — it leads. "Talk to Yulia," not "Contact Sales." She speaks like a deal advisor who can write a tight memo, never like a chatbot.

6. **Editorial restraint.** This is a publication, not a dashboard. Typography carries the design. Full-bleed is welcome but cap content at ~1200px on wide viewports so spacing doesn't drift. Read like *The New Yorker*, not like Robinhood.

7. **Safari toolbar tinting is real.** Never use `position:fixed` full-viewport divs with `background-color` — Safari reads them for toolbar tinting and breaks dark-mode switching. Use `position:absolute` inside a `relative` parent. (See memory: `feedback_safari_toolbar_tinting.md`.)
