## Design Context

### Users
Business owners, brokers, and investors navigating M&A transactions from $300K to mega-cap. They arrive with high stakes — selling a business they built, buying one they'll bet their capital on, or raising funds that determine their trajectory. Context is often mobile-first (checking deals between meetings), shifting to desktop for deep financial analysis. The job: get from "I'm thinking about a deal" to "we closed" with AI-guided intelligence at every gate.

### Brand Personality
**Friendly but authoritative.** Yulia is the brilliant advisor who explains complex deal structures without condescension. The platform feels like a boutique M&A firm that happens to be software — not a SaaS dashboard with finance features bolted on. Three words: **sharp, warm, credible.**

### Emotional Goals
- **Trust** — financial data is sacred, never invented, always sourced
- **Clarity** — complex M&A concepts made accessible without dumbing down
- **Momentum** — every interaction moves the deal forward, never feels like busywork

### Aesthetic Direction

**Grok-minimal with warm paper texture.** Clean, spacious, typographically driven. Rose gold (`#D44A78`) as a surgical accent — functional only, never decorative. Warm cream (`#F8F6F2`) base with subtle film-grain noise, not sterile white. Dark mode is warm charcoal (`#151617`), not blue-black.

**References:** The restraint of Linear, the warmth of Notion, the authority of Bloomberg Terminal (information density when needed, whitespace when not).

**Anti-references:** Generic SaaS dashboards with too many colored cards. Robinhood-style gamification. Anything that looks like a template. Circuit boards, tech-bro gradients, stock photos of handshakes.

**Theme:** Both light and dark, with full Safari toolbar tinting support. Dark mode is a first-class citizen, not an afterthought.

### Typography
- **Headlines:** Sora 800, tight tracking (-0.03 to -0.04em), leading 0.9-1.05
- **Body:** Inter 400-600, 16-18px, leading 1.5-1.6, max ~65ch
- **Financial data:** Inter 500, `font-variant-numeric: tabular-nums` (non-negotiable)
- **No other fonts.** Never Poppins, Montserrat, Roboto, or Caveat.

### Color System
- Accent: `#D44A78` (light), `#E8709A` (dark), `#B03860` (hover)
- Page: `#F8F6F2` (light), `#151617` (dark)
- Cards: `#FFFFFF` / `#2F3133`
- Text: `#1A1C1E` / `#F0F0F3`
- Muted: `#5D5E61` / `rgba(218,218,220,0.8)`
- Never pure `#000000`. Never decorative gradients.

### Motion
- Animate only `transform` and `opacity` (GPU-composited)
- Never `transition: all` — list specific properties
- Honor `prefers-reduced-motion`
- No bounce, no elastic — this is finance
- Micro-interactions: `active:scale-95` on buttons, 200ms tool popups, 300ms theme transitions

### Accessibility
- Target: WCAG 2.1 AA
- All interactive elements keyboard-accessible with visible focus rings
- Color contrast ratios meet AA minimums (4.5:1 text, 3:1 large text/UI)
- Touch targets: minimum 44px on mobile
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` and `prefers-color-scheme`

### Design Principles

1. **Substance over decoration.** Every pixel earns its place. If it doesn't inform, orient, or enable action — remove it. Rose gold is for buttons and active states, not atmosphere.

2. **Financial precision is non-negotiable.** Tabular nums on all number columns. Right-align currency. Never round to clean numbers (47.2%, not 50%). Money in cents, displayed with proper formatting.

3. **Mobile is the real product.** Design for the phone in a broker's pocket first. Desktop is the expanded view, not the other way around. Touch targets 44px minimum, sliders 28px thumb on mobile.

4. **Documents are the deliverable.** PDFs, valuations, CIMs — these are what users pay for. They must look like they came from a boutique advisory firm, not an AI tool. 288 DPI, proper page breaks, cover pages with restrained branding.

5. **Yulia is the interface.** Chat is the front door. Every CTA routes to conversation, not forms or dashboards. The AI doesn't assist — it leads. "Talk to Yulia" not "Contact Sales."
